About me

I trained as an accountant before starting my own business as a software house. In 1992 I founded Demon Internet, the first dial-up Internet provider in Europe. As MD, I built Demon as a start-up and sold it 7 years later for £66m.

Using some of the proceeds, I built Redbus Interhouse and, as Executive Chairman, took this company to a flotation the London Stock Exchange. In subsequent years, I have acted as NED or Advisor to a number of Internet and high-tech companies.

I am currently looking for an interim role as CEO or CTO. I would also be interested in further non-executive directorships.

Founder and Executive Chairman

I founded one of the first modern data centres and quickly took it public, raising over £100m by the end of 2000. The shares rose from 40p to £3.20 in 12 months as we built into seven European countries. In 2001, I was awarded the London Stock Exchange prize for the fastest growing share of the year. In 2002, we had Data Centres in London, Amsterdam, Frankfurt, Milan, Paris, Madrid and Prague.

1998 to 2000

1996 to 1998

Co-Founder and Advisor

From 1996, the IWF has played a major role in preventing illegal child pornography from being distributed via the Internet. I was involved in discussions with government ministers and helped develop a hotline and other systems to assist in the reporting of illegal material.

1994 to 1999

Co-Founder and Director

The LINX was the first UK Internet Exchange. Prior to this, the four ISPs each interconnected directly to each other. With the other founders, we built a scalable Internet Exchange which remains the second largest in the world.

1992 to 1998

Demon Internet Limited

Founder and CEO

I created Demon, the first dial-up Internet provider in Europe, with a business plan for 400 users. With monthly growth rates of 15% plus, the user base grew to around 250,000 over the six years and Demon remained the largest UK dial-up Internet provider in 1998. As CEO, I led a structured sale process and sold to Scottish Telecom for £66m. ($100m).

1985 to 1994

Founder and Managing Director

Demon Systems was a “boutique” software house producing custom systems for financial and networked applications on MSDOS and UNIX platforms.
